# Proposal

## Improving Student Learning Outcomes Through AI-Assisted Study Tools in Undergraduate Computer Science Education  
**Ryan Nguyen**

---

## Background

Artificial intelligence tools such as ChatGPT are becoming increasingly common in academic environments, especially in computer science education. These tools can assist with debugging, explaining concepts, and generating code, which may improve learning efficiency. However, concerns exist about over-reliance on AI and its potential negative impact on students’ critical thinking and problem-solving skills.

Recent studies suggest that AI-assisted learning can improve comprehension when used appropriately, but misuse may reduce deep learning and long-term retention (Kasneci et al., 2023). In computer science courses, where problem-solving and algorithmic thinking are essential, it is important to understand how AI tools affect student performance and learning behaviors.

This project aims to evaluate how AI-assisted tools influence student learning outcomes in undergraduate programming courses, specifically focusing on conceptual understanding, assignment performance, and independent problem-solving ability.

---

## Methodology

This study will use a mixed-methods approach involving both quantitative and qualitative data.

Participants will include undergraduate students enrolled in introductory programming courses at UCF. Data will be collected through surveys, structured programming assignments, and optional follow-up interviews.

Participants will be divided into two groups:
- One group allowed to use AI tools  
- One group without access to AI assistance  

Both groups will complete identical programming tasks designed to assess:
- Debugging ability  
- Algorithm design  
- Code implementation  

### Data Collected
- Task completion time  
- Accuracy of solutions  
- Code quality (readability, efficiency, correctness)  

Surveys will measure student confidence, perceived learning, and frequency of AI usage. Interviews will provide deeper insight into how students interact with AI tools and how it affects their understanding.

The results will be analyzed using statistical comparisons between the two groups to identify significant differences in performance and learning outcomes.

---

## Anticipated Outcomes

1. Students using AI tools may complete assignments more quickly  
2. AI-assisted students may demonstrate mixed levels of conceptual understanding  
3. Increased reliance on AI tools for problem-solving may be observed  
4. Some students may use AI as a learning aid rather than a shortcut, improving comprehension  

---

## Significance

- This research will help educators develop clear guidelines for integrating AI tools into coursework while maintaining academic integrity  
- The study targets computer science educators and undergraduate students  
- The findings will provide insight into how AI affects student learning behavior and offer data-driven recommendations for balancing AI assistance with independent learning
